Learn Arabic

Arabic belongs to the group of languages, which are written from right to left. As almost all of the Western European languages share the Roman script, many Middle Eastern and old Central Asian languages share the Arabic script. Some of these languages include Kurdish, Persian, Dari, Urdu, Pashto, Punjabi (Pakistan), Sindhi, Baluchi, Kashmiri, old Turkish script (languages of modern-day Turkey and other Turkic languages from the Central Asian region).

Today, Arabic is the national language of 20 countries and is widely understood in all Muslim majority countries. Almost every Muslim in the world can at least read Arabic.

Arabic learning for English speakers is generally considered a hard task. I think the hard part is only the Arabic script learning. Once the student learns how to read Arabic, then it’s like learning any other European language with different grammatical structure and rules.

This course is designed in a way that absolute beginners, who have even never seen Arabic alphabet can start and take it to advanced level of Arabic grammar, without need of a physical instructor. The course is of course designed in a step by step progress. First 8 lessons emphasize on learning to read Arabic, which is the most critical phase for most of the English speakers. It is highly recommended not to jump or skip a lesson, even it feels a bit difficult. The every next lesson is based on the previous progress. Be patience, and take your time, 1 day, 2 days or even more on a lesson and only proceed to the next when you have completely understood the current one.

Writing practice is most important and helps a lots in remembering things on long terms basis. It is also recommended to write on separate paper rather than typing with keyboard, although text input boxes are given in some lessons.
